CHEFECHEF.

Voice User Interface (VUI) project focused on drawing out ethical principles and incorportating them in a product the provides emotional and technical support to aspiring chefs on their journies towards confidence.

MY ROLE
As a member of a four-person team, I worked on task management, implemented feedback from concept tests into new conversational features, and led the direction of the branding and supplemental user interface assets. On top of our collective user research and needs-finding, my teammates supported this project through development of storyboards and video prototype.

DESIGN
PHILOSOPHY.

This is a project grounded in empathy. To set us up for success in this goal, our team took time to gather ethical considerations derived from seconary research and user interviews into the ethics of design. They are important to us as individuals, and we listed them out so that we could keep them close to our hearts through the process.

DISCOVER:
AUDIENCE.

With the specific information in hand, our team constructed a persona to further guide our empathy and process.

DEFINE:
CONCEPT TESTING.

To capture the journey that our persona experiences, our team developed a storyboard that detailed specific painpoints and how they may be address with a VUI. From there, we each conducted several concept tests over Zoom that sought to understand emotions and feelings during the process. We then discussed and collected key takeaways.

DELIVER:
PROTOTYPING.

Once we fully understood the expressed and latent painpoints, we were ready to begin development. We moved forward by synthesizing our key insights with our ethical principles in order to deliver three comprehensive app features.

  • 1. Usable and transparent conversations that operate at the user's pace.
  • 2. Constant support and positive affirmations to encourage learning.
  • 3. Personalization options the enable custom experiences and maintain privacy.

VIDEO
PROTOTYPE.

A majority of the work for this project was centered around creating inclusive conversations between user and device. We drew out many scenarios and developed a script for how a typical user may interact with the VUI. Watch our video demo to see this prototpye in action.

NEXT
STEPS.

If we had the opportunity to test our solution in front of a variety of users, we would have loved to delve deeper into specific scenarios that we had initially created conversations for. How can this app specifically help with users who made a mistake? Is it a smooth transition to backtrack? Can this app accurately dictate what must be done, even for visual learners? Does there exist any painpoints in the user flow at any stage of the app? There are so many ways to try and understand how people would like to use this app and where it can fall short. I'm interested in exploring those options.
